COW PEAS


Cowpeas are a versatile legume with various types that are cultivated for different uses.
Each type of cowpea has its own unique characteristics and uses, making them a valuable crop
in different regions around the world

Southern Peas: Also known as field peas, these come in several varieties, including crowder peas, purple hull peas, and cream peas.
They are often grown for their edible pods and seeds. Crowder Peas: These peas are named for their crowded seed arrangement in the pod.

They are typically darker in color and have a strong, earthy flavor. Cream Peas: These have a cream-colored seed and a delicate flavor.
They are less starchy than other cowpea types and have a smoother texture. Clay Peas: Characterized by their reddish-brown color,
clay peas are often used in traditional dishes and can be cooked similarly to other cowpea varieties.

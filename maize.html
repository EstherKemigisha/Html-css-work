Maize

maize isMaize grain is rich in carbohydrates, providing a significant source of energy.
It also contains essential nutrients like fiber, vitamins (such as vitamin B and folate), and minerals (including magnesium and phosphorus).


Dent Corn: Recognized by its indented kernels, dent corn is commonly used for animal feed and industrial products like cornmeal and corn syrup.

Flint Corn: Known for its hard, glassy kernels, flint corn is used for making hominy, polenta, and decorative purposes.

Sweet Corn: This type is harvested while the kernels are still tender and sweet. It's typically consumed fresh, canned, or frozen.

Flour Corn: As the name suggests, flour corn has a soft, starchy texture that's ideal for making corn flour and cornmeal.

Popcorn: A unique variety of flint corn with kernels that pop when heated, turning into the popular snack.

Waxy Corn: Known for its high amylopectin content, waxy corn is used in food
